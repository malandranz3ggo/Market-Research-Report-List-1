<p><p>The gynecological cancer drugs market comprises various treatment types, including alkylating agents, plant alkaloids, anthracyclines, and antitumor antibiotics. Alkylating agents work by damaging DNA to inhibit cancer cell growth. Plant alkaloids, derived from plants, disrupt cell division. Anthracyclines are compounds that interfere with DNA replication and transcription, effectively slowing tumor growth. Antitumor antibiotics target and kill cancer cells by interacting with DNA, preventing their proliferation. Together, these drug categories provide essential therapeutic options for treating gynecological cancers.</p></p>
