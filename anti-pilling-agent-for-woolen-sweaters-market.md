<p><p>The anti-pilling agent market for woolen sweaters is categorized into two main types: ionic and nonionic. Ionic type agents, which include charged molecules, effectively bind to wool fibers, reducing the formation of pills by creating a protective layer. Nonionic type agents, on the other hand, are neutral and work by enhancing the fiber's inherent properties, promoting smoothness and reducing friction among fibers. Both types aim to improve the durability and appearance of woolen garments by minimizing pilling.</p></p>
