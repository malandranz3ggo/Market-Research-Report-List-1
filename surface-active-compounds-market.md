<p><p>The surface-active compounds market encompasses four main types of surfactants. Anionic surfactants, negatively charged, are effective in cleaning and emulsifying applications. Non-ionic surfactants, with no charge, offer stability in various conditions and are used in detergents and cosmetics. Cationic surfactants, positively charged, are popular for their antibacterial properties and are commonly found in fabric softeners and disinfectants. Amphoteric surfactants can carry both positive and negative charges, providing versatility in personal care and industrial applications.</p></p>
