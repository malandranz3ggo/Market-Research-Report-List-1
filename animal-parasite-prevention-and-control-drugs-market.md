<p><p>The animal parasite prevention and control drugs market comprises three main types: topical, injection, and oral medications. Topical medications are applied directly to the skin, effectively targeting external parasites like fleas and ticks. Injection medications provide a systemic approach, delivering active ingredients directly into the bloodstream for long-lasting protection against internal and external parasites. Oral medications involve administering drugs through food or treats, offering an easily accepted method for controlling various parasitic infections in animals. Each category serves specific needs based on parasite type and treatment duration.</p></p>
