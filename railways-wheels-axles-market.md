Rolled wheels and axles are produced through a rolling process, emphasizing cost-effectiveness and uniformity, making them suitable for lighter trains. Conversely, forged wheels and axles are created by heating and shaping metal, resulting in greater strength and durability, ideal for heavy-duty applications. Both types serve essential roles in ensuring the safety, efficiency, and reliability of railway operations.</p></p>
