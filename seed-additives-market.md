<p><p>The seed additives market consists of two primary types: dry form and liquid form. Dry form additives are typically available as powders or granules, offering easy application and storage, while providing essential nutrients, pesticides, or fungicides. Liquid form additives, on the other hand, are solutions that can be mixed with water or fertilizers, facilitating uniform coverage and absorption. Both forms aim to enhance seed performance, improve germination rates, and protect against pests and diseases.</p></p>
